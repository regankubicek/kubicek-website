Who this is for

A thorough evaluation for adults who want real clarity

This practice is designed for adults who want a careful, thoughtful assessment rather than a quick answer or a one-size-fits-all diagnosis. This evaluation is best suited for individuals seeking a comprehensive understanding rather than a brief screening.

This may be helpful for you if…

  • You have been wondering whether ADHD could be part of what you are experiencing.
  • You notice overlapping concerns like anxiety, mood changes, trauma, or feeling overwhelmed.
  • You have tried therapy or medication but still feel like something is missing.
  • You are looking for a clearer understanding of yourself and practical next steps.

What makes this different

Symptoms like distractibility, overwhelm, low motivation, restlessness, and inconsistent follow-through can come from many different sources. This process is designed to understand why symptoms are happening, not just whether a label fits. A core focus is differentiating between ADHD, anxiety, mood, trauma, and personality patterns when symptoms overlap, so the conclusions are accurate and useful.

What to expect from this process

This is not a fast checklist or same-day answer. Instead, the evaluation is designed to take a careful, thoughtful look at your experiences so the conclusions are accurate and genuinely useful for guiding next steps. When appropriate, documentation can support academic, work, or testing accommodations.

The process

Structured, clear, and designed to be useful

The evaluation process is designed to balance depth with practicality so you leave with a clearer understanding of what is going on and what to do next.

01

Brief screener

A short initial review helps determine whether the evaluation is a good fit before scheduling.

02

Interview and testing

The clinical interview and testing are typically completed in one day so the process feels efficient and streamlined.

03

Scoring and integration

After the appointment, test data, clinical history, and observed patterns are carefully reviewed and integrated into the full picture.

04

Report delivery

A comprehensive written report is typically completed within 3 to 4 weeks and includes diagnostic impressions and practical recommendations.

About Dr. Kubicek

Christina Kubicek, Psy.D., is a licensed psychologist specializing in comprehensive psychological assessment for adults. Her work centers on diagnostic clarification in complex or overlapping presentations, including attention and executive functioning concerns, anxiety and mood disorders, trauma-related patterns, and personality dynamics.

She has extensive experience conducting full-battery evaluations in outpatient settings, integrating clinical interview, evidence-based testing, behavioral observations, and collateral information when available. Her assessments emphasize careful differential diagnosis, particularly distinguishing between ADHD, anxiety, mood disorders, trauma effects, and personality patterns when symptoms overlap.

Dr. Kubicek’s approach is both thorough and practical. She prioritizes clear, readable reports that explain not just what the findings are, but why they fit together and how they show up in day-to-day functioning. Recommendations are tailored to be actionable, including therapy approaches, skills-based strategies, and coordination with medication providers when appropriate.

Her style is warm, direct, and grounded. The goal is to leave you with a coherent understanding of your strengths and challenges, and a plan that actually makes sense for your life.
